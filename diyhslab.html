DIY (do-it-yourself) HealthScience LAB

The LAB is a DIY or citizen science focused open source software entity. We are building tools and network protocols to put health science in the hands and hard drives of the people. Especially for the UNhealth-ed and rural people of the world. We have three current DIY healthscience projects:

DIY HealthScience Projects

HealthSpan science

The first test project for the LAB. Peers capture, control and take ownership of their own wearable data. The DIY HealthScience toolkit is used to observe the data and to setup network experiments to continuously aggregate results and outcomes. Experiments use cases examples include, daily step planing and when do I sleep the best? As the range of data entering the network expands these experiments can be put in a holitic context.

Air Quality science

The Luftdaten air quality is worldwide citizen science project to collect particle matter and weather measurements. The HealthScience network can help visualise the data and use it in HealthSpan experiments, Does the pollution level effect my sleep?

Blood Sugar science

Continious blood glucose monitoring is a necessary daily activity for millions of people with diabetes. This technology is now being used by those wishing to understand how their bodies react to activities like intermittent fasting. Is it better to fast for regular short periods or fewer but long periods? The DIY healscience toolkit brings rigour to conducting such experiments.

OPEN SOURCE SOFTWARE

DIY HealthScience Toolkit

A graphical interface onto the Health Science Network. Connects peers to their own data, perform data science and present Oracle reports on the status of experiments on the network. The user experience takes priority to allow everyone access to healthscience knowledge. gitHub

Mobile Dashboards

Visualisation components that deliver the healthscience experiments knowledge to mobile Dapps. github.

Decentralised Machine Learning Protocol

The building of a secure and immutable privacy preserving network of evidence that guarantees reproducibilty and machine computational compatibility. The toolkit uses the SAFEflow library, middle-'data'-ware to connect to data and the network. github.

Oracle

The orcale established consenus on the healthscience evidence network github.

CALE

An evolution alorgrithm that oversees the DIY healthscience toolkit making it ease to manage and interact with the wider HS.network github.

CNRL (computation network reference layer)

Inspired by the referencing work of librarians the CNRL uses cryptogrpaphy and governance peer to peer DAO's (decentralised autonomous organisations) to guarantee the interoperability of data in the network. github.
